Market simulation
Market Simulation Model Simulates annual portfolio movements using Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) — the industry-standard model for asset prices.

Each year the portfolio changes by:
exp((μ − σ²/2) + σ·Z)

where μ is your return rate, σ = 16% (historical S&P 500 volatility), and Z is a random standard normal draw. Expected long-run outcome matches steady growth, but paths swing — as in real markets.

Your Profile

Both Roth and Traditional accounts grow identically — the difference is when you pay tax. Roth: contribute after-tax dollars, withdraw tax-free. Traditional: defer taxes now, pay at your retirement bracket. Choose whichever rate you expect to be lower.
2026 IRS limits: 401k employee contributions $23,500/yr · IRA $7,000/yr. Roth IRA income phaseout begins at $150k (single) / $236k (MFJ). Traditional IRA deductibility limits may apply.
